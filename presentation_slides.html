Problem Statement & Motivation

The Challenge

Depression affects 280 million people worldwide, but traditional assessment methods are subjective and time-consuming.

  • Current Issues: Reliance on self-reports and clinical interviews
  • Detection Delays: Often leads to poor treatment outcomes
  • Accessibility: Need for objective, automated screening tools
  • Our Solution: Analyze speech patterns to detect depression automatically

Why Speech?

Voice contains rich information about psychological state, is non-invasive to collect, and can be processed automatically.

Feature Extraction

Three Main Feature Categories

Spectral Features

  • MFCCs (represents the spectral envelope of speech): 20 coefficients + deltas
  • Log-Mel (captures how energy is distributed across different frequencies): 128 filter bank features
  • Purpose: Capture vocal tract configuration

Prosodic Features (PWP)

  • F0 Contour: The fundamental frequency of the voice
  • Jitter(vibration)/Shimmer: Voice quality measures
  • Voice activity detection (VAD) helps us understand speech rhythm and pausing patterns

Statistical Aggregation

  • Mean, Standard Deviation
  • Percentiles (5th, 25th, 50th, 75th, 95th)
  • Range

Optimization Features

• Parallel processing
• Multi-level caching
• Selective featureextraction
• Memory efficiency

Feature Space Analysis

Dimensionality Reduction

  • PCA: 15 components (85% variance)
  • t-SNE: 2D visualization
  • Purpose: Understand feature relationships

K-means Clustering

  • Clusters: 3 optimal clusters found (depressed, control, mixed)
  • Validation: Silhouette analysis (how similar an object is to its own cluster compared to other clusters) and elbow method (a technique to determine the optimal number of clusters: 3 clusters)
  • Correlation: With depression status

Key Findings

Cluster Analysis Results

Cluster 0: 78.5% Depressed
Cluster 1: 65.2% Control
Cluster 2: Mixed patterns

  • Natural groupings correlate with depression
  • Feature space shows clear separability
  • Prosodic features drive clustering

Feature Importance Analysis

Top 5 Most Important Features

  • std_f0 F0 variability (14.2%)
  • jitter_local Pitch variation (11.8%)
  • shimmer_local Amplitude variation (9.5%)
  • mean_mfcc_1 Spectral tilt (8.7%)
  • vad_ratio Voice activity (7.9%)

Key Insight

60% of top features are prosodic, confirming that depression primarily affects speech rhythm and voice quality.

Clinical Relevance

  • F0 Variability: Reduced pitch variation (monotone speech)
  • Jitter/Shimmer: Irregular vocal fold vibration
  • Voice Quality: Changes in articulation patterns
  • Speaking Patterns: Altered rhythm and timing

Feature Distribution

Prosodic 60%
Spectral 27%
Temporal 13%
